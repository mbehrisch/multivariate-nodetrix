Edge Encodings in Network Visualisation

Participant Information Sheet & Informed Consent

Study at a glance

~15 minutes 4 tasks No prior knowledge needed Desktop / laptop only

Purpose of this study

This study investigates how different visual encodings on edges (connections between nodes) in a network graph affect a person's ability to answer questions about the data. You will interact with a network visualisation of international flight routes and complete a small number of tasks.

This research is conducted as part of a bachelor thesis project at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. Your participation helps determine which visual representations are most effective for different types of analytical questions.

What participation involves

  • You will see a network graph of airport routes and answer 4 tasks.
  • Tasks vary in type: estimating a pattern, finding a specific airport, or navigating the network.
  • Each task has a multiple-choice question or asks you to click directly on a node in the graph.
  • The study takes approximately 10 to 20 minutes.
  • There are no right or wrong answers that affect your reward — all responses are valid.

Data collection & privacy

The following data is collected during the study:

  • Your Prolific participant ID (used only to process your payment; it is not linked to your identity in any analysis).
  • Task responses — which answer you selected and how long you took.
  • Interaction events — which nodes you clicked and in what order.

All data is stored securely on Google Firebase (servers located in the EU). No names, email addresses, or other personally identifiable information are collected. Data will be stored for the duration of the research project and handled in accordance with the GDPR and the VU Amsterdam privacy policy.

Anonymised, aggregated results may be published in the bachelor thesis and any subsequent academic work.

Voluntary participation & withdrawal

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any time by closing your browser. If you withdraw before completing the study, any data collected up to that point will be deleted upon request. To request deletion, contact the researcher using the details below and include your Prolific participant ID.
